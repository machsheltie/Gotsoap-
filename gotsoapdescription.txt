Got Soap? 
A Satirical Thirst Trap Hygiene PSA for the Chronically Unwashed
(or: For Modern Masculinity in Crisis)
Project Intro
Got Soap? is a satirical ad campaign inspired by the iconic “Got Milk?” series—reimagined as a modern thirst-trap PSA for personal hygiene.
This self-initiated project skewers modern dating culture and the rise of low-effort masculinity, using bold visuals and cheeky taglines to call for a return to basic hygiene.
Merging parody, cultural commentary, and aesthetic seduction, the message is clear:
Take the damn shower.
From concept and art direction to layout, compositing, and copywriting, I led every aspect of this project. While AI was used to generate the male models, all direction, styling, and Photoshop execution- including compositing, lighting, and typography- was done entirely by hand.

Poster 1: “A Clean Man Is A Confident Man”
A satirical twist on luxury cologne ads. Our stoic cowboy doesn’t just smell like masculinity—he defines it. The clean design and confident gaze invite viewers to rethink what power, presence, and hygiene should really look like

Poster 2: “He’s Soap-Smoldering”
A play on the classic smolder trope—because what’s hotter than a clean man with self-awareness?
The design uses bold, centered typography and layered shadows to maintain visual clarity across high-contrast skin tones and textured backgrounds.
The line walks the razor edge between thirst trap and PSA—just like he does.

Poster 3: “Unholy”
Some men leave women speechless.
Others just leave a trail of Axe and shame.
But this one?
He washes. Daily. With soap.
UNCOMMON. UNHOLY. Unreasonably hot.
This piece exaggerates the absurdity of low hygiene standards with the drama of a fragrance ad and the conviction of a sermon.
Clean isn’t optional—it’s the bare minimum.

Poster 4: “The Redemption Ad”
He sinned.
He sprayed.
But now? He showers.
This image pokes fun at the pseudo-religious reverence some men assign to deodorant and body spray—treating a squirt of aerosol as holy water for poor hygiene.
Our hero has seen the light (and the loofah).
Presented with revival tent drama and cologne ad seriousness, this mock PSA reframes daily hygiene as not just a habit—but an act of moral reform.
Cleanliness is next to handsomeness.

Poster 5: "Public Thirst Announcement"
“There’s clean… and then there’s ‘Would introduce to Mom without hesitation’ clean.”
This ad leans into understated confidence. No fog machine, no flex — just unbothered, showered, and ready for anything.
Because personal hygiene should be the bare minimum, not a plot twist.
