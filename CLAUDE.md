# Claude implementation brief

Treat the linked documents as executable creative constraints, not inspiration.

Before proposing or building a surface:

1. Name its fictional author: Got Soap?, CWAAA, or the Office of Lather Compliance.
2. State the visual register and the anti-default it rejects.
3. Describe one dominant composition, one interruption, and one designed transition.
4. Check the route against the target PRD and its acceptance criteria.

Do not average the three systems into a tasteful shared design system. Shared engineering primitives
may exist beneath them, but the rendered authors must remain visibly and behaviorally distinct.

When a requirement seems ambiguous, preserve the campaign's commitment and the institution boundary.
Do not “solve” uncertainty with centered containers, equal cards, safe gradients, generic editorial
serifs, pill buttons, or a conventional agency homepage.

The Office is the sharpest constraint: its entire public site is an error-state performance.
Jurisdiction stays deliberately unspecified. Do not add useful agency content to make it feel
complete.

Path-scoped design constraints for the web build also live in `.claude/rules/`.
