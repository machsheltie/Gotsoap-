# prose-critique, applied to *got soap?* campaign copy

This skill was written for **narrative fiction**. You (Sol) are using it to critique
**short-form satirical ad copy**. Read `SKILL.md` for the method, but map it to this domain:

## What carries over verbatim (this is why you're reading it)
- **The adversarial mindset (SKILL.md "The Adversarial Mindset").** Find what doesn't work.
  The writer already believes the line lands; challenge that. "Well done" without digging is
  worse than silence — it creates false confidence.
- **What makes a good finding (SKILL.md "What Makes a Good Finding").** Specific (name the
  string), reasoned (say what it *costs* — a lost laugh, a broken wink, a dead CTA), directable
  (the writer knows what to do next), non-obvious. "This could be punchier" is not a finding.
- **Communicating impact / severity.** Lead with the lines that damage the read — a joke that
  explains itself, a line that could come from any brand, a mis-addressed wink. Minor polish follows.
- **Critique the execution, not the committed premise.** The satire, the two-author system, the
  voice bible's rules are *decided*. Don't relitigate them; judge whether the line executes them.

## Which resources matter here (and which to skip)
- **`resources/prose.md`** — line-level quality, rhythm, repetition, show-vs-tell. **Primary.**
  Ad copy is all line-level. This is your main lens.
- **`resources/voice.md`** — voice consistency, subtext, drift. **Primary.** Map "narrator voice"
  → the campaign register (and CWAAA's deadpan). Does every line sound like Stacey, unapologetic?
- **`resources/antipatterns.md`** — AI writing tells. **Primary.** This *is* the Stepford test's
  evidence base — the "could any brand say this" failure, in detail.
- `resources/structure.md`, `character.md`, `continuity.md` — fiction plot/arc/timeline. **Skip**
  for copy; there's no plot. (The one exception: "continuity" maps loosely to keeping the dual-address
  signer consistent — the man signs, not her.)
- `resources/analyze.py` — a fiction-baseline mechanical script. **Ignore** here; not meaningful
  for 7 short strings.

## The one substitution
Where SKILL.md says "reader experience," read **"the dual-address reader"**: every line winks at
*him* (the accused) or *her* (the distributor), ideally both. A finding that a line doesn't know
who it's winking at is a first-class severity finding — that's this campaign's version of "confused POV."

## Your bar is still the voice bible
This skill sharpens *how you critique*; `docs/copy/voice-bible.md` is *what you critique against*.
Run the Stepford test (voice-bible) using this skill's finding discipline. Consensus is a line you
and Claude would both defend to Stacey — not one you're tired of arguing over.
