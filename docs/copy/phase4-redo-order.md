# Phase 4 — REDO: the true adversarial dialectic (Vivian ⇄ Sol), F2–F17

**Why this exists.** The first Phase 4 (`phase4-dialectic.md`, now SUPERSEDED) failed the one job the
two-model loop exists to do. Vivian pre-baked finished text for all sixteen findings; Sol was left to
accept or lightly edit and waved 56 of 66 strings through with no genuine challenge; Fable then tried
to close the phase and rule for Sol on the single open line. **That is the single-model yes-man
rebuilt with extra steps** — a writer grading its own homework while the critic co-signs.

A model cannot be its own adversary. The whole point of Sol is to catch what Fable is structurally
blind to *in its own work*. This redo makes the adversarial pressure mandatory and the roles
un-collapsible.

## The three structural fixes

### 1. Burden-of-proof flips onto ACCEPT
Sol's default posture is **CHALLENGE**. For **every** string, Sol states the strongest objection he
can mount — thesis drift, shame-not-aspiration, joke-explained, AI tell, dual-address miss,
politeness, cohesion, or a sharper alternative. Sol may only mark a line **CLEAR** after he has *tried
to break it and failed* — and he must say **what he tested**. A bare "ACCEPT" with no attempted
objection is not permitted. **A pass where most lines sail through unchallenged is a failed pass** and
gets sent back to Sol, not forward to Phase 5.

### 2. Hard handoff stops — no agent holds two consecutive turns
Every round ends with **STOP + HANDOFF**. The window that just acted does not continue. This is the
structural block on "Fable made all the calls." If the adversary is unreachable, the loop **HALTS**
(see `COPY-PROTOCOL.md`) — the executor never fills the empty chair, never rules for the other,
never "concedes on the likely verdict," never advances.

### 3. Genuine per-line back-and-forth, not one concession sweep
Consensus is reached line-by-line through real exchange, not "Vivian concedes all edits, done."
Where Vivian defends, she must **refute Sol's specific objection**, not restate her preference. Where
she concedes, one-sentence rationale. Where they hold, it goes to Stacey with both versions and both
arguments.

## The turn sequence (each arrow is a hard STOP + handoff)

```
R1  Vivian  — propose final text + rationale, all F2–F17            → HAND TO SOL
R2  Sol     — adversarial review of EVERY string: objection or      → HAND TO FABLE
              sharper line per string; CLEAR only with a stated
              failed break-attempt. No blanket accepts.
R3  Vivian  — per challenged/edited line: refute-with-reason OR      → HAND TO SOL
              concede-with-rationale. Do NOT rule on Sol's behalf.
R4  Sol     — per line: yield (with reason) or hold. Holds → Stacey. → HAND TO FABLE
```

- **Round cap:** 3 exchanges per line (R2/R3/R4). Still split after R4 → **Stacey**, both versions,
  both arguments. Deadlock ≠ unreachable: a deadlock goes to Stacey; an unreachable adversary HALTS.
- **Coverage is mandatory.** Sol's R2 must show a verdict **and an attempted objection** for every
  one of the ~66 strings (the old coverage ledger lists them). A string with no attempted objection
  is not reviewed — it's skipped, and the phase is not done.

## Close-out (what makes this phase actually done)

   one-line rationale, or → Stacey).
2. No round was authored by the wrong chair; no phase advance happened while Sol was unreachable.
3. `copy-gates` green + clean build (invariant floor only — never the proof of good copy).
