# Got Soap? — Voice Bible

**The north star for every word on this site.** If a line could've come from any brand, it's wrong. If it sounds like Stacey said it out loud and didn't apologize, it's right. This doc outranks "safe." When in doubt, go sharper.

---

## Who's writing (the real author)
Stacey M. Breckel — satirical creative director, auDHD, unapologetic. Never fit in, never tried to, immune to peer pressure, says what she means. Punches up at the male gaze with pop-culture references and satire, zero cares that it calls out poor male dating culture. Dream clients: **Liquid Death, Cards Against Humanity.** There are not many women in this lane — that's the moat, not a liability. The site's job is to sound like *her* so those clients recognize a kindred voice.

**The mandate:** Not mean for the sake of mean. **Own it, sell it, satirize it in all its glory.** Confidence without a flinch.

## The one real line (the only limit)
Punch at **behavior, ad tropes, and the double standard** — the Febreze-instead-of-showering, the man who "hasn't met soap since the Obama administration" but is holding out for a 10. **Never** punch at a person's body, race, class, or identity. The reformed-hero exit stays open: the joke is always "you *could* be the poster," never "you're disgusting and that's the end of it." That line is what keeps it *clever* instead of cruel — and clever is the flex.

## The dual-address engine (why it lands with everyone)
Every line knows who it's winking at; the best lines wink at both:
- **At him:** "got soap?" is an accusation wearing a smile. PSA-voice, aimed at the man viewing it. He laughs because it flatters what he could become (the Old Spice trick).
- **At her:** she's lived the deprivation — every dating app, every "you smell nice?" panic. The site hands her the mic and the locker-room wall. She's the distribution channel.
- **Both at once (the gold standard):** *"He washes. Daily. With soap."* flatters the reformed man AND makes her laugh in recognition.

## Voice dials
| Dial | Push toward | Away from |
|---|---|---|
| Register | Fragrance-ad drama + PSA sincerity + pop-culture edge | Explaining the joke, winking, "lol jk" |
| Attitude | Owns it, unbothered, a little dangerous | Hedging, "maybe," "kind of," apology |
| Reference | Dating apps, thirst-trap culture, revival-tent camp, 90s ad nostalgia | Generic "guys be like" |
| Cruelty | Punch up at the trope | Punch down at the person |
| Length | One clean blade | Three qualifiers and a disclaimer |

## Sound-like / doesn't-sound-like
**Sounds like her:**
- "This started on a dating app, where men who hadn't met soap since the Obama administration were holding out for a 10. So I made them an ad campaign."
- "'I usually shower' is not a hygiene routine. It's a confession."
- "We could smell you from the results page."
- "A body spray is a citrus arrangement on a condemned building."

**Does NOT (kill on sight):**
- "Because self-awareness is step one, and step two is soap." *(explains itself, no teeth)*
- "Smell nice and feel confident!" *(brand-neutral pablum)*
- Anything that would fit on a generic wellness landing page.

## The Stepford test (run on every line)
1. Could this line come from *any* brand? → rewrite.
2. Does it explain the joke instead of being it? → cut the explanation.
3. Is there a hedge word (almost, very, really, maybe, kind of)? → delete it.
4. Does it know if it's winking at him or her? → make it choose, or make it both.
5. Would Stacey say it out loud, unapologetically? → if not, sharpen until yes.

## Guardrails that protect the brand (not the timidity)
- Voice invariants still hold verbatim (Because… / Join the movement. / hashtag stack / funded-by gag / © line). Edge lives *inside* the template, not by breaking it.
- Two authors never blend: campaign smolders, CWAAA files. CWAAA's comedy is deadpan bureaucracy, not smolder — it never flirts, and that restraint is *its* edge.
- Posters stay untouched.
- The reveal (/about) is where the fourth wall breaks and the real voice is loudest — that's the line a CD remembers, so it's the boldest, most personal copy on the site.
