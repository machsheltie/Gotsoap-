# Reader-sim evidence — three blind reads (Phase 2)

**Method.** Three `reader-sim` agents read the **rendered text of all 16 pages** (extracted from a
fresh `dist/`, in visitor order). Each was given **a persona and nothing else** — no
`CAMPAIGN-INTENT`, no voice bible, no specs. **Blind by design:** a reader told what to feel
reports the feeling they were told to have. Read-only. This is evidence, not judgment.

**Status:** this is the copy-side equivalent of the machine gates. Sol and Fable may *argue with a
reading*, with reasons. Neither may wave one away.

---

## The three readers

- **Maya, 34** — on the apps three years. Arrived via a friend: *"SCREAMING. this is every man on hinge."*
- **Dylan, 29** — showers "usually," proud of the beard, body spray as a shortcut, ghosted last month and doesn't know why. Arrived via a friend: *"lol bro take this quiz."*
- **Priya, 41** — CD at an irreverent DTC brand, 50 portfolios a month, hunting for a point of view. 45-second attention budget.

---

## UNANIMOUS FINDINGS (all three, independently — the strongest evidence in this document)

### 1. The nine Recovery Case Files are the site's biggest self-inflicted wound
- **Maya:** "Ten of them. TEN… I hit the wall at RC-039." Skimmed four.
- **Dylan:** "Nine of them is too many. I got the pattern by four… **If you cut it to five I'd have read all five.**" Started reading only the bold STATUS lines.
- **Priya:** "Nine iterations of one shape… **it reads like someone hit 'generate six more in this voice,'** and I say that with confidence because that is exactly what the uniformity of the arc looks like. **Cut four, keep five, and the section doubles in power.**"

All three independently hit the wall in the same place. Priya also: *"The case files should have been the smallest section and are the biggest. That's a self-editing gap, and self-editing is what I'm actually assessing when I look at a spec piece — because a spec piece has no client to blame."*

### 2. RC-071 (Sean) is quoted on the home page and is the weakest of the nine
- **Maya:** "the home page is leading with a leftover." **Priya:** "Wrong pick."

### 3. The reassurance lines are a flinch — but see the CRITICAL DISTINCTION below
- **Maya (lectured):** *"The men are not the problem. The men are, in most cases, lovely."* → "Who is this for? Not me. That's the site checking over its shoulder." And *"Nobody's beyond redemption"* → "I didn't ask."
- **Priya:** "Three places, and they're all the same flinch: **reassurance.**" — *"this is a maintenance problem, not a character one"* → "the verdict page spends four sentences insulting him and then takes it back. **Let him sit in it.**" Plus *"Nobody's beyond redemption"* → "Two hugs in one paragraph."

### 4. `/about` asks permission, and both women flagged it
- **Priya:** *"There aren't many women working this lane."* → "**a permission slip**… she's explaining why she's allowed to be in the room instead of already being the loudest person in it." Keep the second half ("That's exactly why you want one") — it's a claim.
- **Maya:** *"I'm not here to be nice about it… unapologetic."* → "**Telling me you're unapologetic *is* an apology.** The work already proved it. That sentence un-proves it a little."

---

## THE CRITICAL DISTINCTION (emerged only from triangulation)

Maya and Priya say **cut the reassurance**. Dylan — the man the campaign must convert — says the
opposite about *aspiration*. These look identical and are not:

- **Aspiration = the conversion engine. KEEP.** Dylan's exact buy-in line:
  > *"The good news is almost insulting: soap costs four dollars, works on contact, and **asks nothing of your personality**."*
  > "**This is the line where I bought in.** Because it's the only line that lets me keep being who I am and still fix the thing. Everything else diagnosed me. That one gave me an exit that isn't humiliating."
- **Apology = the flinch. CUT.** "not a character one," "Nobody's beyond redemption," "the men are lovely."

Dylan converted on **aspiration**, not on reassurance. The apologies bought nothing from him and cost
credibility with both women. **Keep the four-dollar exit; delete the hugs.**

Dylan is also emphatic on the mechanism:
> "**Aspiration, and it's not close, and that's the site's actual achievement.** The posters don't shame me… The shame is in the case files, and **the shame is what made me skim. The aspiration is what made me stay.** If this site were only the case files I'd have closed it in ninety seconds and felt fine about myself."

## THE CENTRAL DIVERGENCE (the strategic fork — for the dialectic)

**`/about`'s anger:** two readers want more of it, one is repelled by it.

- **Maya:** *"men who hadn't met soap since the Obama administration… looked, and presumably smelled, like damp disappointment"* → "**That anger is the product.** The site keeps putting it in a manila folder." She wants it everywhere.
- **Priya:** "a **grievance with a receipt**. It's not a strategy deck." It is a major reason she'd hire. → "**The work is meaner than the person publishing it is willing to admit, and every time she covers for it, the piece gets smaller.**"
- **Dylan:** "**This is where my back actually went up.** Not the quiz. Not the verdict. Here… 'damp disappointment' is the first line on the site that's about winning an argument instead of landing a joke… **the site converts me, and then /about un-converts me by about ten percent.** If Marcus asked me at that moment I'd have said 'it's funny but she kinda hates us.'"

**Arbiter's read (argue with it):** `/about` is **not a conversion surface** — it's the reveal and
the portfolio. Its audience is Priya (the hire) and Maya (the ally). Dylan only arrives there
*already converted* — he'd already showered. Losing 10% of a converted man to win the CD and the
woman may be the correct trade. **The anger belongs at `/about` precisely because that's where the
readers are the CD and the woman.** But note Dylan's distinction: he objects to the sentence that's
"about winning an argument instead of landing a joke" — not to meanness per se.

## WHAT'S MISSING (the highest-value gaps)

### 1. **There is no seat for her at the quiz — vector 2 (woman → man) is BROKEN**
- **Maya:** "The quiz says *'You've smelled him. Are you him?'*… Then I open the quiz and every question is aimed at him. **I'm not the respondent. I'm the reason.**"
- She answered as her exes anyway: *"A pond. A specific pond. It has a name"* — "That's *literally* him."
- The site **knows** women are the delivery system — Gary's daughter, Tyler's mom, Marcus's coworker forwarded it — "the site *knows* women are the delivery system **and then doesn't hand me the envelope**."
- The pledge has the mechanic (*"Sign it — or send it to the man who should"*); the quiz doesn't.
- **Would she send the site to a man? NO.** "The site is too kind to him and too long to survive being sent as an accusation — he'd read the case files, feel understood, and text me 'lol so true' without changing anything."
- **Her product idea, unprompted:** "If there were an **'issue him a citation'** thing that arrived cold, with a case number and no context — **I would use that weapon monthly.**"
- What she wanted: "to take it *about him*. Seven questions, my answers, and a verdict I could screenshot with the caption 'date three, 2024.' **That version I'd have finished and posted.**"

### 2. Vector 3 (man → man) **WORKS** — and the mechanic is remarkable
- **Dylan:** "**I would not send it to Marcus.** Marcus sent it to me. Sending it back is admitting it landed. I'd send it to **Kevin**, from work, who is objectively worse than me, with 'lol bro take this quiz.' Word for word what Marcus sent me."
- > "**The site propagates through guys refusing to admit it hit them.** You built a thing men forward specifically to not talk about it. That's why it'd actually spread."
- **Do not break this.** It is the campaign's live viral mechanic and it was found, not designed.

### 3. Priya's list (the buyer's view)
- **Evidence it scales** — "a system that has only ever run once is a hypothesis." A bulletin archive, a chapter newsletter, a named field assessor with a grudge.
- **Anything off-site** — "two million concerned women and zero evidence anyone outside the site has ever touched it… Put a photo of one of these taped above a real urinal and the whole thing gains a dimension."
- **A CWAAA antagonist** — "The org has no opposition. Everyone in the case files converts. A hostile letter to the editor, a Form CW-1 rejected, a chapter that went rogue. **Friction is what makes a bit into a universe.**"
- **A price and a process** — "/about says 'Want a campaign like this?' and then gives me an email… I'm a buyer and she left the buyer's question unanswered."

## ⚠ THE HARD FLAG — the case-file roster may punch DOWN (Maya, flagged "hard")

The campaign's one non-negotiable is: punch at **behavior**, never at body/race/class/identity.
Maya's structural reading of the roster:

> "Derek does lawn care and his truck smells like a hamper. 'Kaelthas,' 22, is a basement streamer.
> Big Ron is 71 and smells old. Brayden's sheets are 'seasoned.' Now look at **Marcus** — ninety-dollar
> cologne, articulate, gets the most dignified write-up and the classiest punchline. And **Chad**, who
> is hot, is the one who ends up CERTIFIED.
>
> So the roster reads: **working-class guy, gamer, old man = the smell. Affluent guy = a correctable
> oversight. Hot guy = passes.** I don't think that was on purpose, but it's on the page… **Big Ron is
> also the only one whose *body* is the joke rather than his behavior — age-smell is not a choice the
> way skipping the shower is.** He's also the best-written one, which makes it worse, not better.
> Flagging it hard because the site's entire defense is 'we're punching at behavior,' and three of
> these ten are punching at a demographic."

**This is a direct hit on the campaign's own stated line.** The unanimous "cut to five" is the
opportunity to fix the roster's class/age pattern in the same edit. (Note: Priya independently found
Big Ron "genuinely tender… earns its place" — they disagree on the *line*, not on the *pattern*.)

**Others, converged:**
- *"Three were still warm. We do not wish to discuss the three."* — all three. "It works because of the **refusal**."
- *"The tenth asked to leave the study."* — "the whole joke is in the tenth."

## THE VERDICTS THAT MATTER

**Does the CTA work?** **Yes.** Dylan: "I'll shower tomorrow. It's a work-from-home day and I'll do it
anyway, and I'll feel a little stupid about why, and I won't tell anybody… **the hoodie's getting
washed this weekend and *that* I'm sure about.**" Caused by *"a rumor about a shower you didn't take"*
— it gave him a hypothesis for the ghosting he'd been explaining away for a month.

**Would it get her hired?** **Yes — today.** Priya: "I'd reach out. **Today, in the fast lane.**" For
**the CWAAA layer, not the posters**: "the press releases, the form, the findings, the error states.
That's a specific and rare skill… **I have three briefs a year that need exactly that and I can never
staff them.**" Senior/lead rates, campaign-world scope.
> Her Slack message: *"ok this Got Soap thing is a fake nonprofit that files paperwork about men who don't shower and the press releases are better written than our last three campaigns… Pulling her in for the [redacted] brief."*

**Her closing note — the sentence the whole review turns on:**
> "It's held back by two things, both fixable, both about nerve: **it over-produces** (nine case files
> where five would kill) and **it apologizes at exactly the moments it should press**… Cut a third of
> the crisis page, delete every reassurance, and this stops being a very good portfolio piece and
> starts being a calling card. As it stands: I'd reach out. **I'd also, in the first call, ask her why
> she keeps saying sorry.**"

**Maya's:** "It's a fake nonprofit with a real grudge and a genuinely great writer, and it's about
**30% too generous to the people it's about**… Every time the site gets close to saying *you have been
coasting on our labor and our standards while smelling like a hamper,* it hands the guy a loofah and
tells him he's lovely."
