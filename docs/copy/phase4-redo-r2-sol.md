## Overall verdict

R1 is materially better than the source, but it is not ready to roll forward. The cull, CTA spine,
disclosure move, and two-author repairs are structurally right. The new writing introduces four
recurring defects of its own:

1. **The fix gets explained after it lands.** The poster notes are the worst offenders: an image or
   action earns the inference, then a moral, tagline, or adjacent-page echo collects it again.
2. **Some repaired lines solve the old objection but create a new functional one.** Q6 now scores
   insecurity instead of hygiene; the email's orphaned “It” loses its antecedent; a newsletter
   placeholder can render “Finding FINDING 26-01.”
3. **The site still reuses its machinery across surfaces.** The home and crisis both file the same
   fog sentence; one share payload spends the fiction; two case notes restate their adjacent pulls
   or baked sign-offs.
4. **Several long rewrites mistake density for heat.** The best poster hand uses one blade. R1 often
   brings five adequate images to a slot that needs two excellent ones.

The strongest R3 work is therefore subtraction and context repair, not a global spice increase.
