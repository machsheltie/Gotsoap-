# Part A, Round 2 — Sol's adversarial review

## Verdict

R1 identifies the right failure but does not fully remove it. The scarcity cut is right, and the
grudge/triplicate material finally sells in the voice that made the site. Two pieces still change
costumes: the surviving case-study sentence remains agency taxonomy, and the pitch ends by turning
the reader into a prospect again. **Three slot decisions CLEAR; two are CHALLENGED.**

## Slot 1 — `about.why[0]`: CLEAR unchanged

**Break-attempt:** “model-adjacent 10” could be accused of repeating the male gaze or rating a
woman's body. The objection fails because the sentence assigns that demand to the entitled man and
immediately invoices him for what he offers in return. The target is the double standard, not the
woman; Maya's “actual thesis of my dating life” response confirms the address landed. “So I made
them an ad campaign” is causal action, not a tidy summary. No sales-pivot language enters.

## Slot 2 — `about.why[1]`: CLEAR unchanged

**Break-attempt:** “Made clean… Made effort… Aimed…” is a three-beat syntactic pattern, and the
placement inventory is another three. The objection fails because the beats are not interchangeable
service claims: each advances the demonstrated strategy from status, to desire, to distribution.
The last clause, “they've been living the research,” performs the woman's authority instead of
naming her POV. Cutting the sequence would cost the Got Milk? mechanism the page is licensed to
reveal.

## Slot 3 — `about.caseStudy[2].body`: CHALLENGE

Deleting **“Built like it's real, because that's the whole point”** removes Priya's tidy-summary
tell. It does not clear the sentence left behind:

> Three registers — porcelain, smoke, marble — a fictional sponsor with its own identity and its
> own deadpan voice, and a campaign that smolders and files paperwork in the same breath.

**Strongest objection:** the line still spends most of its length naming the system's components in
case-study language: *registers, sponsor, identity, voice, campaign*. Only “smolders and files
paperwork” performs. The reader has already seen the registers and met the sponsor, so this taxonomy
does work the page has performed for her. The cost is the same costume change Priya identified, one
sentence earlier: the moral is gone, but the deck voice remains.

**Counter-text:**

> Porcelain. Smoke. Marble. Then I incorporated the grudge. It files paperwork now.

**Mechanism:** retain the precise register inventory, then turn the sponsor system into comic action
and consequence. “Incorporated” earns the nonprofit without explaining it; the final sentence lets
CWAAA's behavior prove its own identity and voice. No generic capability noun survives.

## Slot 4 — `about.pitch.body`: CHALLENGE

The paragraph has one clear keeper, two cuts, and two edits.

### “I'm Stacey M. Breckel… is one hand: mine.” — EDIT

**Break-attempt on the three-item inventory:** *campaign / fake nonprofit / paperwork* resembles the
services triad Priya rejected. That objection fails. These are concrete artifacts the reader just
encountered, and the hero-copy comparison is an earned, specific flex; none is a promise about a
category of work. The inventory is proof.

The actual defect is grammatical and tonal: **“everything… is one hand”** is a compressed résumé
claim, not the idiom. “Came from one hand” makes authorship the consequence of the proof. Splitting
the name into its own sentence also removes the introductory throat-clear rhythm.

### “Nobody commissioned it.” — CUT

This is defining by negation in its pure form. It asks the absence of a client to certify initiative,
which makes the reader consider the work's lack of commission instead of its excess of authorship.
The dating-app cause already proves Stacey made the campaign without permission. Repeating the fact
as a negative is a permission cough, and it lowers the sale to “look what I did unpaid.”

### “A dating app made me mad… funded entirely by spite and a Photoshop license.” — EDIT

The first clause works: grievance becomes national apparatus. The funding tail overplays the seam.
A Photoshop license cannot fund anything, and Photoshop was named in the adjacent Execution item;
repeating the tool sells software operation where the page should sell the mind that invented the
crisis. Stop on the disproportionate action: **“A dating app made me mad, so I declared a national
hygiene crisis.”**

### “I file my grudges in triplicate.” — CLEAR

**Break-attempt:** this could be CWAAA register bleeding into Stacey's real voice, or a self-conscious
quotation of the site's running paperwork bit. The objection fails at this designed seam. `/about`
is the licensed fourth-wall surface, and the sentence makes her authorship visible by turning a real
temperament into the fictional institution's behavior. It performs Priya's “grievance with a
receipt” response without stating “women bring a different POV.” This is the proof-backed flex the
scarcity line wanted to be.

### “Imagine what I'd do with a brief.” — CUT

Calling the close a dare does not change its function. It is the familiar portfolio move *imagine
what we could do for you*, and it turns the reader into a client in the final sentence — Maya's exact
temperature drop. The existing heading **“Want a campaign like this?”**, contact, tagline, and
Behance control already perform the sale structurally. A second ask is not conversion; it is the
pitch explaining that it is a pitch. Worse, it overwrites the sharper triplicate button.

**Counter-text for the full slot:**

> I'm Stacey M. Breckel. Everything you just read — the campaign, the fake nonprofit, the paperwork
> that out-writes most brands' hero copy — came from one hand: mine. A dating app made me mad, so I
> declared a national hygiene crisis. I file my grudges in triplicate.

**Mechanism:** identify the product, attach the claim to visible proof, perform the originating POV
as disproportionate action, and stop on the line only this maker could own. The sale remains; the
sales-deck close does not.

## Slot 5 — scarcity line: CLEAR cut, no replacement

**Break-attempt:** the voice bible calls the scarcity a moat, so deleting its explicit statement
