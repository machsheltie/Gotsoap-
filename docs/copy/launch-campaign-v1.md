## 1. What this campaign is actually selling

Two products, one funnel — never confuse them:

1. **The bit** — a committed, deadpan hygiene movement people *share because it's funny*. This is the top of funnel and the whole vibe.
2. **The designer** — Stacey Breckel / Hope2 Studio. The real conversion. Every viral share is a portfolio impression; the reveal page turns a laugh into an inquiry.

**The campaign objective:** make women laugh and share → capture emails via the Pledge → route creative directors to `/about` and `hope2studio@yahoo.com`.

**Do not** run this as a "personal brand" launch. Run it as the launch of *a movement*. The portfolio is the punchline, never the pitch. (Liquid Death rule: entertain 100%, sell in the fine print.)

## 3. Positioning & messaging pillars

**One-line positioning:** *A public thirst announcement — the hygiene PSA for men, told by the women standing too close to them.*

Three message pillars (rotate; never run all three in one post):
1. **Clean is the flex.** ("Smell like effort." / "Cleanliness is next to hotness.") — the seductive, aspirational pillar.
2. **The fog isn't fooling anyone.** ("Axe is not an exorcism." / "Deodorant isn't divine intervention.") — the roast pillar.
3. **Join the movement.** (Sniff Test, Pledge, CWAAA fiction) — the participation pillar.

**Tone guardrails (from CLAUDE.md — enforce in every caption):**
- Punch at *behavior and Axe-fog culture*, never a person's identity, body, race, or class.
- Keep the reformed-hero exit open — the joke is "you could be the poster," not "you're disgusting."
- Commit to the fiction. No "lol just kidding" in captions. The footer/reveal carries that load.

## 4. Launch arc (4 phases)

### Phase 0 — Prime (T‑minus 2 weeks)
- Stand up `@gotsoap` on IG + TikTok + Threads. Bio in voice (see §7). Behance gallery already live — link it.
- Seed 3–4 poster teasers as *cropped mysteries* (steam only, headline only) — no site link yet. Caption device: "Because…" cliffhangers.
- Warm the CD audience: one Threads/LinkedIn post from Stacey's own account teasing "a campaign I built to see if I could sell a movement that doesn't exist."

### Phase 1 — Launch week (site goes live)
- **Day 1:** The reveal-the-hero drop. Fogged-mirror hero as a video (wipe-to-reveal), link in bio → `/`. All five posters as a carousel.
- **Day 2:** Sniff Test goes live → "What's your verdict?" reel. This is the sharable engine — push hardest here.
- **Day 3:** CWAAA "stats" carousel (the /crisis facts). Deadpan nonprofit energy.
- **Day 4:** The Pledge → "Two million concerned women. One demand." Drive email capture.
- **Day 5:** The Redemption angle (Poster 4) + reformed-man UGC prompt.
- **Day 6–7:** Repost the best shares/duets; let the audience carry it.

### Phase 2 — Sustain (weeks 2–6)
- 3–4 posts/week. Rotate the three pillars. Reply in-character as the movement.
- Weekly "Field Assessment" prompt (drive Sniff Test). Weekly CWAAA "bulletin" (drive fiction).
- One CD-facing post/week on LinkedIn/Behance: the *making-of*, not the memes.

### Phase 3 — Compound (week 6+ / v2 triggers)
- Poster drop #6 as a social event → newsletter send to the pledge list (mechanics §9).
- Turn on caption generator + live pledge counter (v2) and make *those* the new shareable.

## 9. Risk & moderation (from PRD §11)
- **Misread as sincere shaming:** lead with the reformed-hero and "clean is the flex" pillars, not pure roast. If a thread turns mean in the replies, the movement account redirects to redemption ("we're not angry, we're a nonprofit — we don't have the budget for angry").
- **Fiction mistaken for a real org:** the footer parody line + "Funded by Concerned Women Against Axe Abuse" is self-evidently absurd; the reveal page states intent plainly. Never post a CWAAA "stat" without the account's obvious-parody context.
- **Never** nominate/tag real strangers or targets (mechanics §7 is share-a-link-only; the account models that). No pile-ons.
- **AI-provenance questions:** answer plainly with the /about disclosure — models generated with openart.ai, all ad-craft by hand. It's a strength, not a liability.
