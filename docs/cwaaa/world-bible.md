# CWAAA world bible

## Canonical identity

**Name:** Concerned Women Against Axe Abuse
**Initialism:** CWAAA
**Established:** 2024
**Entity:** fictional advocacy nonprofit / national coalition
**Mission:** make routine soap-and-water hygiene the expected baseline and document the social costs
of substituting fragrance for washing.

CWAAA is modest, competent, patient, and real within the fiction. The comedy comes from applying
serious nonprofit procedure to an absurdly low bar. It does not come from misspellings, broken
design, corruption, cruelty, or institutional incompetence.

## Three-system law

- **Got Soap? campaigns.**
- **CWAAA advocates and files.**
- **The Office of Lather Compliance regulates.**

CWAAA may cite or link to the Office. It never claims that the Office is a CWAAA department. Office
directives, containment notices, terminal IDs, repeat-access records, and “Please remain available”
language never appear as CWAAA-authored content.

## Canonical world facts

- What began as one book club's informal grievance log became a national coalition.
- CWAAA's scale line is: “Two million concerned women. Chapters in all fifty states. One demand.”
- Its pledge program is the **Lather Pledge**, identified as **Form CW-1 — Declaration of Intent to
  Lather**.
- Its washcloth-ribbon program is **Tie One On For Suds**.
- Its seal combines a soap bar with a barred aerosol can.
- Its public findings use numbered `FINDING YY-NN` labels.
- Its recovery case files use `RC-NNN` identifiers and protect subject names within the fiction.
- The man makes the first-person pledge. The woman may witness, recruit, share, or refer him.
- The Lather Pledge is also available on Got Soap? through the same contract and Buttondown audience.

Existing finding text and case-file copy remain in `site/src/content/copy.ts` until extracted. They
must pass the established copy protocol before substantive revision.

## Voice

CWAAA writes like a capable nonprofit whose staff have done this before:

- procedural but readable;
- earnest without sentimentality;
- firm without menace;
- dry because the procedure is specific, not because it chases jokes;
- helpful without behaving like a customer-support SaaS product.

CWAAA uses `files`, `findings`, `declarations`, `case files`, `chapters`, `program`, `referral`, and
`published record`. It does not use `containment`, `terminal`, `observed`, `regulatory standard`, or
`access denied`; those belong to the Office.

## Emotional temperature

CWAAA does not frighten. It builds collective relief: someone finally wrote this down, put a seal on
it, and made the expectation legible. Its certainty can be funny, but its relationship to the
visitor is advocacy, not surveillance.

## Canonical programs

### The Lather Pledge

Form CW-1 records a man's declaration to shower and use soap. `contracts/pledge.v1.json` governs
both public implementations. CWAAA owns the program even when Got Soap? hosts a campaign edition.

### Tie One On For Suds

The washcloth ribbon is a visible marker of support and a pledge funnel. It can appear on vehicles,
gym bags, doorknobs, print pieces, and event materials. It is affectionate nonprofit material
culture, not a government insignia.

### Findings

Findings parody annual-report evidence through ruled tables, citations, and precise labels. Do not
add fake scientific apparatus beyond what the joke can support. Facts are clearly fictional in
quiet legal copy.

### Recovery Case Files

Case files are reform stories, not criminal records. They may be generated or shared as campaign
artifacts. They do not imply real legal standing or government custody.

## Legal and ethical frame

- State clearly in accessible legal/footer copy that CWAAA is fictional satire.
- Do not imply affiliation with MADD, Axe/Unilever, a public-health organization, or a government.
- Never publish real personal allegations as case files.
- Do not imitate a specific live government seal or use a jurisdictional name.
- Humor targets low-effort masculinity and fragrance substitution, not protected classes or people
  with medical barriers to hygiene.

## Open configuration

The final CWAAA domain, Got Soap? domain, and Office domain are owner decisions. Keep cross-site URLs
empty and conditional until assigned.
