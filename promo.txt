Got Soap? 
A Satirical Thirst Trap Hygiene PSA for the Chronically Unwashed
(or: For Modern Masculinity in Crisis)
Project Intro
Got Soap? is a satirical ad campaign inspired by the iconic “Got Milk?” series—reimagined as a modern thirst-trap PSA for personal hygiene.
This self-initiated project skewers modern dating culture and the rise of low-effort masculinity, using bold visuals and cheeky taglines to call for a return to basic hygiene.
Merging parody, cultural commentary, and aesthetic seduction, the message is clear:
Take the damn shower.
From concept and art direction to layout, compositing, and copywriting, I led every aspect of this project. While AI was used to generate the male models, all direction, styling, and Photoshop execution- including compositing, lighting, and typography- was done entirely by hand.

Process Breakdown
Concept & Messaging
The tone walks a tightrope: visually seductive but thematically irreverent. I intentionally paired high-contrast thirst trap imagery with slogan copywriting designed to provoke, amuse, and push boundaries. This blend of eye-catching visuals and sharp wit challenges traditional norms around masculinity and hygiene through deliberate satire.

I intentionally leaned into a mix of bold, retro-inspired fonts and aggressive styling effects to match the over-the-top masculinity and campy seriousness of vintage fragrance ads.

Color & Composition
The tonal range of the images demanded careful text placement and intentional color edits. Adjustments to skin shadows allowed my primary CTAs to return to black, enhancing visual cohesion and message clarity. Each layout was approached like an ad campaign—equal parts branding, visual punch, and legibility optimization.
Special Readability Solutions
To maintain visual cohesion without sacrificing legibility:
I used light dodging under "Join the Movement" and other key phrases where shadowing interfered with contrast.
I tested multiple font combinations, color overlays, and styles—often finding that legible solutions undermined the tone. So instead, I made micro-adjustments to lighting and texture, allowing the original styling to remain intact.
Gradient overlays were applied strategically in difficult zones to subtly boost contrast.
In tight areas, I split text into multiple layers to better control spacing and movement across image elements.
This was less about plug-and-play design and more like precision styling—balancing function, form, and message with intent.
Final Asset
Final campaign assets are presented side by side. In addition to the full posters, I also created minimalist versions featuring only the headline (“Got Soap?”), CTA, and hashtag stack. In a real-world execution, these could incorporate QR codes, campaign-specific landing pages, or interactive social elements—further bridging parody with performance.
